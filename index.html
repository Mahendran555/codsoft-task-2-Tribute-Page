Learn about the life of A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, India’s first president:

  • A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, (born Oct. 15, 1931, Rameswaram, India—died July 27, 2015,)
  • After graduating from the Madras Institute of Technology, Kalam played a leading role in the
    development of India’s missile and nuclear weapons programs.
  • He planned a program that produced a number of successful missiles,
    helping earn him the nickname “Missile Man.
  • Beginning in the early 1990s, he also served as scientific adviser to the government.
  • prominent role in India’s 1998 nuclear weapons tests established Kalam as a national hero.
  • In 2002 the Hindu nationalist (Hindutva) National Democratic Alliance nominated Kalam,
    a Muslim, to succeed outgoing President K.R.Narayanan.
  • Kalam easily won the elections in 2002, and in the largely ceremonial post he sought to use science and technology
    to transform India into a developed country.
  • In 2007 he was succeeded by Pratibha Patil, the country’s first woman president.

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam BR was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman
who served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007.
